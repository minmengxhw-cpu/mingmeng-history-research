“QUIT CHINA WEEK”
20,000 Chinese Have
Already Signed Petition
To Truman
The inauguration meeting of the
‘Singapore Chinese Federation for
Peace and Democracy in China.’
an organisation recently formed by
the China Democratic League, the
Hokkien Association, the Chinese
Committee of the Federation of the
Trades Unions and many other
Chinese social bodies, will be held
on the 27th instant at 2 p.m. at the
Victoria Memorial Hall.
Mr. Ng Aik Huan, Chairman of
the Preparatory Committee, will
preside and office-bearers will be
elected at the meeting. Mr. Tan
Kah Kee, Chinese Community
Leader, will be present and. will
deliver a speech.
In connection with the “Quit
China Week,” its organiser, the
‘Federation for Peace and Democ-
racy in China’ has _ issued invita-
tions to various community leaders
to a tea party on the 26th instant
at 3 p.m. at the Mayfair Associa-
tion, No. 112, Robinson Road.
It is understood that up to
yesterday, over 20,000 signatures
have been obtained in a letter to
President Truman  urging the
immediate withdrawal of Ameri-
can forces from China,
The campaign will reach its
climax on the 27th instant when
the inauguration meeting of the
‘Singapore Chinese Federation for
Peace and Democracy in China’,
will be held.
