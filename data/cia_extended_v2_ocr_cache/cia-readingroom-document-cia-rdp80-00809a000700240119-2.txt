BORISOV, Nikolay Andreyevich, Maj Gen Engr-Tech Serv, Hero of Socialist Labor 


Died on 13 September 1955 after a long, serious illness. BORISOV joined 
the Communist Party in 1931; he began working as an apprentice metalsmith, and 
after finishing a "worker's faculty" [special training courses] at the Moscow 
Machine-Building Institute he held several technical positions and was chief 
engineer at a machine-building plant. 


Since 1938, BORISOV worked as Deputy Chairman of 
Deputy Heepee ok GORD (Gos ckoncmneemeSe ay SSR, State moan Wenicgiog 
USSR), and as a responsible worker in the Council of Ministers USSR; uur- 
ing World War II he was responsible tor the production and supply of ammuni- 


tion (boyepripasy) to the Soviet Army, and also for organizing the production 
of new weapons and combat equipment; his decorations inclyded the title Hero 
of Socialist Labor, two Orders of Lenin, two Orders of Labor Red Banner, the 
Order of Kutuzov First and Second Classes, the Order of the "Badge of Honor," 
and medals of the Soviet Union. Signed: a group of comrades. 


The administration and social organizations of Gosplan USSR and Gos- 
ekonomkomissiya USSR also published an announcement of BORISOV's death. (Mos- 
cow, Pravda, 15 Sep 55)
