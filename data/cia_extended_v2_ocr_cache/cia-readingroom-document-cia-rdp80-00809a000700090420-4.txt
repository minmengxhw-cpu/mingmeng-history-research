marked a decisive turn in the People's Democracies. The working class seized 
power and the People's Democracies became proletarian dictatorships. This 
development went hand in hand with the consolidation of the socialist sector 
in the national economy; the dislodging of capitalist elements fron industry, 


banking, credit, transportation, and wholesale commerce; and the initiation of 
planned economy. 


Execution of a Two-Year Plan in Bulgaria and Czechoslovakia and of a Three 
Year Plan in Poland and Hungary was begun in1947. The relationship between 
workers and work changed, and socialist competition received a powerful impetus. 
The productivity of labor, as well as production, increased at a pace unimagin- 
able under zspitalism. War damage was eliminated. At the same time, the work-
