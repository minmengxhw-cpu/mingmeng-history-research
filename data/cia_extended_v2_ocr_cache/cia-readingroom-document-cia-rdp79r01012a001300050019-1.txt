COMMENTS ON ONE DRAFT PAPER NIB=45 ON YUGOSLAVIA: 


1, The statement in this paragraph contains two distinct situations 
which should be clearly divorced. A situation including a build-up of 
Satellite capabilities should not be treated in the same frame as "an 
apparent decline in Satellite aggressiveness;" for to Belgrade there is only 
an academic distinction between apparent intentions and capabilities insofar 
as the orbit is concerned, 


2, Here it might be well to clearly define what is meant by the term 
"est." If NATO is synonomous with the term, the statement is not entirely 
accurate, Yugoslav rapprochement and desire for a mutual defense pact with 
Greece and Turkey (ewer constitutes “ee one glaring exception to this gen- 
eralization, , 


With respect to the motives underlying Relgrade's anti-Church mani- 
festations, it is unlikely that the principal reason for the Vatican break 
was an attempt to offend Western public opinion for the sake of asserting 
its independence. Since the break it has become apparent that the action 
was largely a reaction to Vatican intransigence and a specific act designed 
to reduce the power of the Church over its hierarchy in Yugoslavia. 


3, The assumption underlying the statement that Yugoslavia will seek 
to use cooperation with Greece and Turkey as a "device to isolate Italy and 
put pressure on Italy, and also will seek to establish its own primacy over 
we Greece as a Balkan power," is questionable. For one thing, the term "to 
isolate Italy" needs clarification. For example, it is doubtful that Yugo- 
slevia entertains the misconception that it can isolate Italy from France, / 
or its other European allies, On the other hand, exclusion of Italy from 7 
a Balkan alliance, or at the very least, the restriction of Italian in- 
fluence in Greece and Albania is a realistic program. 


4. There are no indications of Yugoslav efforts to encourage Titoism 
in China through dedlings with China. Also, the concept of Yugoslavia in- 
creasing its efforts to build-up a "third force" group of small nations 
conflicts with the general evaluation made in paragraph 1 to the effect that 
Yugoslavia will increase its Western orientation as the power of Satellite 
armies increase. Yugoslavia's efforts to support a "third force" will 
probably be matched or exceeded by its attempts to establish closer working 
relationships with various left-wing groups in Europe and Asia,
